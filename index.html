About Me

I am a first year computer science PhD student at the Kahlert School of Computing & Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute at the University of Utah. I am supervised by Dr. Mike Kirby (opens new window) and Dr. Shandian Zhe (opens new window). I am broadly interested in scientific machine learning, physics-informed machine learning, probabilistic machine learning, and theoretical foundations of machine learning. I completed my undergraduate degree in computer science from the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities under the supervision of Dr. Vipin Kumar (opens new window). My honors thesis was titled "Deep Learning to Address Data Sparsity in Climate Change Monitoring (opens new window)".
